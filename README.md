## Purpose
Among the top 10 leading causes of death worldwide, Alzheimer's is the only one that can't be slowed, prevented, or cured. Research to-date has focused on understanding neuron pathways, medicinal treatment, and behavior assessment. These approaches are either invasive, expensive, or catch the disease at a late stage.

Because the brain's neural circuits and vascular network are coupled, **changes in brain blood flow may predate symptoms**. To examine this hypothesis, **our approach measures brain blood flow at various states of neurodegeneration**. If proven, an individual's vascular response to stimulation can be measured without incision, and such diagnostic procedures would be widely deployable.
